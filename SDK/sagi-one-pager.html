Side A — the foundation

The compute engine

deep-tech · distributed search

A SETI- and Bitcoin-style network that borrows idle compute from volunteers and aims it at one question: what's the next, better way to build a model?

  • →Nodes run bets — candidate model designs, e.g. how layers and connections are wired.
  • →Each bet is trained and tested locally, then scored on accuracy, efficiency and power.
  • →Scores are published to open leaderboards — the search is transparent and competitive.
What contributors get Tokens for the compute they donate — redeemable for model usage, or co-ownership of what the network finds if SAGI cracks the next level.
Side B — the scale

The application layer

growth · the human edge

The job: make joining the search effortless and, for most people, fun. Two ways in — one quiet, one playful.

  • ▶Passive — it just runs in the background and donates compute while you do nothing.
  • ◆Active — you play, and your choices steer the search. Pokémon GO, but the play trains AI.
  • ＊The active layer supplies the signals you can't automate: preference judgments, scouting promising designs, red-teaming.
The roadmap of the layer Launch with one game, the platform locked. Later, open it up so anyone can build games and tools on top — every one of them feeding SAGI.

Hard problems we're taking on

The difficulty is the moat.

These are the questions everyone in decentralized AI is fighting over. Naming them is how the pitch stays credible — and each one we solve is defensibility.

trust

Verification & anti-cheat

Once tokens have value, nodes are incentivized to fake work. We score redundantly and validate before rewarding — proving compute is the core research problem.

physics

Compute inequality

A single datacenter GPU dwarfs thousands of phones. So we route heavy evaluation to capable nodes and use the crowd for breadth and judgment, not raw training.

reach

Browser-first, not app-store

App stores block background compute for external networks. Running in the browser via WebGPU sidesteps that — and lets anyone join the swarm from a link.

design

A real human edge

Humans lose at guessing numbers but win at taste, scouting and adversarial creativity. The game must harvest exactly those — not skin a random search.
